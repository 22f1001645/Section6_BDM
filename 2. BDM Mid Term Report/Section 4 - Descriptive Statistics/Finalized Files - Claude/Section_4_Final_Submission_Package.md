### **Q1: "Explain your approach to computing coefficient of variation."**
**A:** "CV was computed as (standard deviation / mean) √ó 100% for each product's daily sales volume. For example, a product with mean daily sales of 10 units and standard deviation 4.5 units has CV = (4.5/10) √ó 100% = 45%, indicating moderate demand volatility. This metric enables comparability across products with different volume scales‚Äîlow-volume premium items versus high-volume staples‚Äîby normalizing variance relative to mean."

### **Q2: "Why does Fruits category have lower CV (142.325%) than Vegetables (224.752%) despite higher absolute volatility?"**
**A:** "This paradox reflects different volatility drivers. Fruits' moderate CV reflects managed volatility through inventory buffers and premium brand strategies that partially absorb cost shocks.
